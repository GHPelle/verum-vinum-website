Our philosophy

The craft and
love of wine

Our portfolio consists of both classic wines and innovative producers with the common denominator that the wines are of the highest quality.

Among our producers you will find both the winemaker who does their utmost to let the grape and terroir express themselves fully without too much interference, and producers who with finesse in both the vineyard and winery put their own stamp on the wine.

We are very proud of the carefully selected producers we work with as they fully reflect our own wine philosophy. The craftsmanship and love of wine combined with quality and style that represents its terroir has been one of our primary starting points.

"Having the opportunity to offer you as a customer wines from these fantastic producers is what drives us"

Serralunga d'Alba, Piemonte

Ettore Germano

"Four generations of men in love with their vineyard." This quote encapsulates the pride and love that Ettore Germano has for his grapes and wines.

Read more

Starting as early as 1856, the family's land has tripled to the total area the family holds today. In addition to the 10 hectares in Serralunga d'Alba, where the production also takes place, they also have 9 hectares in Cigliè, Alta Langa.

Today the company is run by Sergio Germano, who inherited it from his father Ettore Germano, who started winemaking under the name Ettore Germano in 1975. After graduating in oenology in 1985, Sergio gained experience at three major wine producers before choosing to return home and help his father with the production.

In 1988 they bottled 5,000 bottles split between Barolo, Dolcetto and Barbera. The major breakthrough came in 1993 when they harvested significantly more grapes, which were then bottled.

Ettore Germano today holds land in the renowned vineyards of Prapò, Lazzarito, Cerretta and Vigna Rionda.

Neive, Piemonte

Fontanabianca

A wine should only be called a quality wine if it reflects the taste of its terroir. This is the winemaking philosophy at Fontanabianca.

Read more

Fontanabianca is located in Piemonte, or more precisely in Neive. They describe it themselves as "in the heart of Barbaresco's terroir". The focus is on the red wines, and above all Barbaresco, rich in elegance and perfume.

Today it is the third-generation winemaker Matteo Pola who runs the winery, having taken over from his father Aldo, whose father Franco founded Fontanabianca in 1969. Fontana has for several decades produced wines clearly identified with the hills of Neive and the magnificent wine region that is Piemonte.

The calcareous clay soils and medium-structured earth yield the perfect grapes for producing perfumed, elegant and full-bodied wines. All wines are made with 100% single-varietal grapes.

Verduno, Piemonte

Diego Morra

The Morra family's history goes back to the mid-1900s when the family acquired their first small parcel of a vineyard.

Read more

Today the family has approximately 30 hectares of vineyards in Verduno, La Morra and Roddi. Diego has produced wine under his own name since 2009, but the first vintage of Barolo did not come until 2011. In 2013 he changed the labels and the world was introduced to his three M's for the first time.

The M's stand for Mosca, which is the family's house located in the heart of Monvigliero, which also represents one of the M's and is the most important vineyard for the family. The final M is the family name Morra.

In recent years Diego has truly earned a prominent place among many wine lovers. Not least with his Barolo Monvigliero and San Lorenzo, but also with his fantastic Pelaverga.

Roero, Piemonte

Alberto Oggero

Hearing Alberto talk about his love for the hills of Roero along with his respect for Arneis and Nebbiolo is wonderful.

Read more

When Alberto decided that wine would be his life's work and he would create his own vineyard, he had a few goals in mind. He wanted to create wines that reflected terroir in the best possible way, breathe new life into old vines, and make wines that were fully drinkable upon release.

Nebbiolo should be a wine you could drink on any day and with dinner without needing to turn to rustic dishes, while also seeing Arneis as a type of Nebbiolo Bianco.

The challenge of working with an incredibly acclaimed grape and an underrated grape motivates Alberto, who has now created three different Nebbiolo and two different Arneis wines, all sharing the common trait of clearly reflecting their terroir.

Available wines:
  • Valle dei Lunghi

Monforte d'Alba, Piemonte

Réva

Réva is a dynamic and young wine house in the Barolo region with a team full of energy that wants to create something significant.

Read more

"We want to create wines that are unique, precise, and that truly make you feel that the wines are made with respect for the grapes and their terroir." This is how Daniele introduced Réva to us when we met him for the first time.

One of Réva's goals is also to produce wines that are drinkable immediately but that can also handle several years of cellaring. They have vineyards in six different areas: Monforte d'Alba, Barolo, Novello, Serralunga d'Alba, Grinzane Cavour and Roddino. Réva holds hectares in the three sought-after vineyards of Cannubi, Ravera and Lazzarito.

In addition to their red wines, Réva makes a very unique white blend for Piemonte. The wine Langhe Bianco Grey is made from 70% Sauvignon Gris and 30% Sauvignon Blanc -- try it!

La Morra, Piemonte

Lapo Berti

Lapo grew up in a small village in Tuscany and from a young age developed an interest in wine and winemaking.

Read more

His other great passion in life is traveling, and he naturally chose to combine his two great interests. Lapo now has over 20 years of winemaking experience, having worked in Italy, Australia, France (Burgundy), USA (Oregon) and New Zealand (Otago).

Since 2012 he has been working on his own Barolo project in La Morra, as Lapo, like many of us, loves Nebbiolo. He doesn't own any vineyards but leases a parcel in the Fossati vineyard and buys grapes from friends in the La Morra area.

Lapo adds nothing to his wines except minimal sulfur. He produces approximately 3,500 bottles in total and works exclusively with Nebbiolo. Today Lapo has 4 different wines: a Pet Nat, a Langhe Nebbiolo and two Barolo, where the crown jewel is his Barolo Fossati.

Available wines:
  • Langhe Nebbiolo
  • Barolo del Comune di La Morra
  • Barolo Fossati
San Casciano in Val di Pesa, Toscana

Cigliano di Sopra

Behind the name we find the two young winemakers Matteo Vaccari and Maddalena Fucile.

Read more

After completing their studies in winemaking and oenology in 2017, they both worked at various producers in Tuscany before heading to the USA to work with the star duo Sashi Moorman and Rajat Parr at their California and Oregon based wineries Domaine de la Cote, Sandhi, Pence Vineyards and Eveningland.

Eventually they landed back in Tuscany, more precisely in northern Chianti, where Maddalena's parents had taken over a farm with old vineyards that they both restored, introducing organic farming and some experiments with biodynamic cultivation. Today they have about 7 hectares with soil of sand, clay, limestone and large old river stones.

All harvesting is naturally done by hand and the wines ferment with native yeast using 30% whole clusters. Aged in French tonneaux and barriques. The wine is not filtered and the use of sulfites is kept to a minimum.

Available wines:
  • Chianti Classico

San Casciano in Val di Pesa, Toscana

Mori Concetta

The estate was purchased in 1965 by Massimo Becattelli's grandparents and was then inherited by his mother Concetta and his father Mario.

Read more

In 1970 the first vines were planted and in 1971 the first olive trees, and between 1971 and 1972 the winery was built to begin producing Chianti Classico.

When Massimo's mother passed away in 2014, he inherited the entire estate that had been a large part of his life, where he continues to produce a world-class Chianti Classico and their award-winning olive oil. When Massimo took over completely, he began by replacing some of the old vines with new ones and started working entirely organically, and since 2015 all products are organically certified.

In total, Mori Concetta has a 1.2-hectare vineyard in northwestern Chianti Classico, more precisely in San Casciano in Val di Pesa.

Available wines:
  • Morino Chianti Classico
Montalcino, Toscana

Villa i Cipressi

In the mid-1970s Villa i Cipressi was born when son Hubert Ciacci chose to expand his father's small honey farm.

Read more

Hubert's dream and goal was to start growing and producing high-quality wines and olive oils. The major turning point came in the 1990s when Hubert purchased more land specifically for growing olives and grapes. Today, in addition to Hubert himself, his wife Patrizia and their sons Dario and Federico also work at the estate.

In 2000 the family began producing Brunello di Montalcino and Rosso di Montalcino. The family has 3.5 hectares of Sangiovese in two locations in Montalcino, one in the south and one in the west. The grapes from the two vineyards with completely different soils and microclimates are then blended to create a richer and higher quality wine.

From the very best grapes of the vineyards, they also produce Zebras Brunello di Montalcino.

Available wines:
  • Rosso di Montalcino
  • Brunello di Montalcino
  • Zebras Brunello di Montalcino

France

Moussy, Champagne

Victor Charlot

The Charlot family has been growing grapes since 1877 in Moussy, Vallée de la Marne, and Victor is now the sixth-generation winegrower.

Read more

However, it wasn't until 1960 that the family began producing Champagne themselves, and not until 2000 that they handled all aspects of production in-house. The family's vineyards total 6.5 hectares and consist of 70% Pinot Meunier and 30% Chardonnay.

That the wines clearly reflect their terroir and true nature is paramount for Victor and his wife Charlotte. While wanting to carry forward his family's heritage, Victor also continues to break new ground with new cuvées beyond those that have existed in the family since the beginning.

All wines have always been fermented in stainless steel tanks without malolactic fermentation, but Victor has now started experimenting with 6 barriques, so more exciting cuvées are on the way!

Vougeot, Bourgogne

Domaine Christian Clerget

In 1930 the winery was founded by Jeanne Clerget. Justine is the fourth-generation winemaker.

Read more

The family's vineyards total 6 hectares around the village of Vougeot with vines in Chambolle-Musigny, Morey-Saint-Denis, Vougeot and Vosne-Romanée.

Since Justine took over, she has constantly strived to improve and draw on the experiences of previous generations. One of her first initiatives was to convert the vineyards to organic farming, and in 2017 they were certified by Ecocert.

Harvesting is always done manually by a small team of experienced workers. The grapes always macerate for at least three weeks and vinification is done without added yeast and with as little intervention as possible. The aging period is then 18 months in barrels before bottling.

Chassagne-Montrachet, Bourgogne

Domaine Coffinet-Duvernay

Coffinet-Duvernay was born in 1989 following the marriage of Laura Coffinet and Philippe Duvernay, but the family estate dates back to the 1860s.

Read more

Today it is the son Bastien (5th generation winemaker) who has the main responsibility for the family's estates and winery. Bastien decided in 2012 after completing his studies to work with his mother and father, and in 2018 he took over the main responsibility.

He says himself that he is a big fan of his father's wines and wants to keep as much as possible as it was before. But given the climate conditions in recent years, he had to review his approach.

Bastien works organically in the vineyards without being certified, and today the family has 6.5 hectares of vines exclusively spread across 14 Chassagne-Montrachet appellations.

Pommard, Bourgogne

Domaine Michel Rebourgeon

The domaine has a long history stretching all the way back to 1540.

Read more

Michel took over the domaine in 1964 from his mother and father with a total of 3 hectares of vineyards and has over the years managed to purchase 1.25 hectares more, and today has vineyards in Pommard, Beaune and Volnay.

Today William has the main responsibility. William's hard work in the vineyards combined with his approach in the winery has truly elevated the wines to a new level! By reducing extraction, the elegance has become more pronounced, and William has never wanted to compromise on freshness.

You should be able to drink the wines young if you wish, but also be able to cellar them for 15 years without any problem. After working organically for several years, they decided in 2020 to also become certified.

Morancé, Beaujolais

Domaine La Famille K

In 2018 Benoit Merchier and his family took over a 12-hectare vineyard from a family that had been growing grapes there for 7 generations.

Read more

After living a stressful city life, it was time to retrain and fulfill the dream of working with their passion.

The goal of La Famille K was to take care of the old vines in an environmentally friendly way and produce unpretentious wines of high quality that are accessible to everyone. Sulfur levels are kept to a minimum, and since 2022 La Famille K is organically certified.

Their wines come in 4 different families: L'Héritière, La Mère, Le Père and L'Oncle. The first three families consist of a red wine made from 100% Gamay and a white wine made from 100% Chardonnay, but with different vinification methods and styles.

Arbois, Jura

Domaine Villet

Domaine Villet is found in Arbois, Jura, with origins dating back to 1900 and a strong tradition of natural winemaking.

Read more

Today the domaine is run by Nathanael Villet, who continues to develop the family's philosophy with a focus on craftsmanship, sustainability and respect for the terroir. The vineyards have been farmed without chemicals since 1988 and are organically certified.

With a total of 5.7 hectares divided among 7 vineyards consisting of approximately 50% Chardonnay, 20% Savagnin, 15% Poulsard, 8% Pinot Noir and 7% Trousseau.

The portfolio is broad and consists of both ouillé and sous voile wines as well as sparkling, pet nat, Macvin du Jura and Vin Jaune.

Condrieu, Northern Rhône

Clos de la Bonnette

In 1992 the estate was purchased by the Guiller family after Isabelle had her eye on it for several years.

Read more

The estate and its vineyards were abandoned after World War II until the family took over. The vineyards had never been sprayed, which Isabelle preserved by continuing to work entirely organically and obtaining certification. It wasn't until 2009 that the family vinified a vintage themselves.

The vineyards at the domaine are situated on incredibly beautiful terraces on one side of a valley overlooking the Rhône. The quality of the fruit is so high that Marcel Guigal wanted to buy their grapes and sign a contract with them. Thankfully, they politely declined.

Due to narrow terraces and steep slopes, the site is unsuitable for mechanical harvesters, tractors or horses. So everything must be done by hand. In total they have about 5 hectares of vineyards in Condrieu and Côte-Rôtie.

Available wines:
  • Condrieu Légende Bonnetta
  • Cisselande VdF
  • Syrah Vieilles Vignes
  • Côte-Rôtie Prenelle

Portugal, Germany & Austria

Sangalhos, Bairrada

Quinta das Bágeiras

In 1989 Mario Sergio Nuno started his project, which he named Quinta das Bágeiras.

Read more

He inherited the vineyards from his grandfather Fausto and his maternal grandfather Armando, and together with his father Abel they began bottling their own wines after previously producing wine to sell in bulk.

His conviction was simple: he wanted his wines to be produced using traditional methods, without ignoring the constant development in viticulture and oenology. That is why all their grapes are hand-picked, their red wines are produced in lagares without added yeast, and why their sparkling wines have no residual sugar.

Mario Sergio's belief is that quality means nothing without identity.

Vadio

In the village of Poutena, located in the Bairrada region, we find Luis Patrão and Eduarda Dias who together created Vadio.

Read more

Vadio started in 2005 with Luis at the helm as winemaker. When they started in 2005, the couple had 0.5 hectares of vineyard and today they have a total of 7.5 hectares.

Luis worked for 13 years in winemaking at the producer Herdade do Esporão. In addition to Vadio, he is also the head winemaker at Herdade de Coelheiros.

São João da Pesqueira, Douro

Quinta do Javali

In 1982 the family purchased their first vineyard due to their love of port wine and began producing 2-3 barrels per year.

Read more

In 2000 the son-in-law was asked if he wanted to take over and Quinta do Javali was started. Quinta do Javali is the world's smallest port wine producer, making port exclusively from their own grapes which they vinify and age themselves.

In 2016 they began experimenting with biodynamic farming in the oldest vineyard Clos Fonte do Santos, with vines up to 90 years old and around 30 different grape varieties. It proved to be a success, and after that they began working biodynamically in all vineyards.

Since 2023 the son Antonio is 100% responsible for the still wines. Antonio's philosophy is to truly showcase their vineyards and their terroir. "You should be able to feel the place of origin." Undoubtedly one of the most interesting producers in Douro!

Traben-Trarbach, Mosel

Weingut Martin Müllen

In the magical Mosel Valley we find the fantastic Riesling producer Martin Müllen.

Read more

Since 1991 Martin has been growing Riesling in vineyards including Kröver Paradies, Kröver Letterlay, Kröver Kirchlay and Kröver Stefensberg.

Every vineyard has its own character and magic, which is reflected in Martin's wide range of Riesling wines. The intention is to produce Riesling wines with finesse and a rich flavour palette that requires more than one sip to reveal the wine's secrets.

It is with great passion and love for their wines that Martin and his son Jonas carefully tend to their vines. The harvest is done entirely by hand to ensure that only grapes of the highest quality are used.

Wosendorf, Wachau

Rudi Pichler

The Pichler family has been based in the village of Wosendorf since 1731 and purchased their original cellar in 1884.

Read more

Today the meticulous Rudolf Pichler IV, known as Rudi, cultivates fifteen hectares of vineyard including some of the recognised "grands crus" in Wachau. The vineyards consist of 65% Grüner Veltliner, 30% Riesling and 5% split between Weissburgunder and Roter Veltliner.

These are wines that combine precision with serious depth, accessible when young but with many layers of flavour waiting to unfold. Rudi always wants complexity in the wines over concentration.

About us

Verum Vinum

Verum Vinum was founded by three wine geeks who also happen to be very good friends, two of whom are trained sommeliers.

The idea behind the company is to spread what we love, namely great wines.

We operate in regions close to our hearts and wine styles that you can find plenty of in our own wine cellars.

The producers we have chosen to work with are very dedicated to their craft and have a wine philosophy very similar to our own.

We have a close relationship and have become good friends with all our producers, which means we often have visits from our producers in Sweden and can offer our customers tastings with the producers as well as winemaker dinners.

Like many other wine lovers, we have a passion for Burgundy and Piedmont, but we are constantly looking for new regions and producers that fit our portfolio and philosophy "Honest wines from Small-scale producers".

Our goal is to always be a flexible and reliable partner/supplier to our customers.
